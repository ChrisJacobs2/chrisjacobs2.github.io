Don

A "simple, booth-lined Japanese restaurant specializing in hot entrees like donburi, teriyaki & ramen." Don is a small Japanese restaurant located on Tate Street. It's a great place to go if you're in the mood for ramen, udon, or soups. They also have rice bowls and other entrees. I have personally been there, and it has a great atmosphere. The food is also very good. I ate the beef udon, but I have been told the ramen is delicious as well. The Chef is a very nice person, and the food is reasonably priced.

Jimmy John's

"Counter-serve chain specializing in sub & club sandwiches, plus signature potato chips." I have also personally eaten here, as well as ordered delivery. The sandwiches are both much better than subway, and are relatively the same price. They also have a rewards system, so if you love sandwiches, this is a great place to go. Also located on Tate Street. Beware: Jimmy John's is a chain restaurant, so if you're looking for something "local", there are better options. That said, for a corporation, their mottos and sense of humor are pretty funny. And the people who work at this specific establishment are very nice.

Yum Yum Better Ice Cream

Old school ice cream parlor with a vintage vibe, offering a variety of flavors & toppings. They also have hot dogs and other hot food as well. I get ice cream here sometimes in the summer. Some people don't like it, but I think it tastes great. It's not exactly economical at $3 a scoop, but it's a better deal than most places and the ice cream is better. My favorite is pineapple, and they have about 20ish flavors. You can pick from a cup or a cone, and they give you a fair amount. Make sure to get napkins. They also accept credit card payment, despite the google maps description. Also, their specialty is definitely ice cream. The hot dogs are okay, and they look rather icky.
